The Internet in Argentina in Numbers

Internet speed evolution

100 Mbps average speed in 2025, compared to just 0.05 Mbps in 2000

Internet access growth

92% household penetration in 2025, up from 7% in 2000

Public Wi-Fi Expansion

Over 20,000 public Wi-Fi hotspots available nationwide by 2025.

Mobile Internet Adoption

Over 85% of Argentinians access the internet via mobile devices as of 2025

Fiber Optic Growth

More than 5 million households connected to fiber-to-the-home (FTTH) networks by 2025

E-commerce Surge

Online sales exceeded USD 15 billion in 2023, reflecting a rapid shift to digital shopping

Key facts about the Internet in Argentina

The Cybercafé Era

Argentina’s early internet growth was heavily driven by cybercafés, which made access affordable for many during the late 1990s and early 2000s

Argentina’s Internet Gateway

Located in the coastal town of Las Toninas, Buenos Aires Province, this hub receives nearly all of Argentina’s submarine fiber optic cables, acting as the primary entry point for international internet traffic.

National Satellite Infrastructure

The government-owned company ARSAT manages national satellites and infrastructure to improve connectivity, especially in rural areas
